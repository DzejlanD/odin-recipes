Pizza Recipe

Perfect Homemade Margherita-Style Pizza

Crust: A well-hydrated dough (around 65% water) made from bread flour or Italian “00” flour. It should rise slowly to develop flavor—overnight in the fridge is ideal. When baked, it becomes airy with a slightly charred edge and a tender bite.

Sauce: A fresh tomato sauce made from crushed San Marzano tomatoes, a small pinch of salt, and a drizzle of olive oil. No cooking needed—this keeps the flavor bright and clean.

Cheese: Fresh mozzarella, sliced or torn into small pieces so it melts evenly without making the pizza watery. A sprinkle of grated Parmesan or Pecorino adds depth.

Toppings: Keep it simple: fresh basil leaves, a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil, maybe a pinch of dried oregano if you want a classic pizzeria touch.

Finishing Touch: Bake at the highest heat possible (home ovens: 250–300°C / 475–550°F) on a pizza stone or steel. The high heat creates blistered edges, melted cheese, and a perfectly crisp bottom.

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 300 g bread flour or Tipo “00” flour
  • 195 ml warm water
  • 1 tsp salt
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp dry yeast (or 7 g fresh yeast)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil (optional)

For the sauce:

  • 1 can (400 g) crushed tomatoes (preferably San Marzano)
  • 1–2 pinches of salt
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small pinch of dried oregano (optional)

For the toppings:

  • 200–250 g fresh mozzarella
  • 30 g grated Parmesan (optional)
  • Fresh basil leaves (5–10)
  • Olive oil for drizzling
  • Pinch of black pepper (optional)

Detailed Steps

1. Make the Dough

  1. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl mix the warm water, sugar and yeast. Let sit 5–10 minutes until foamy.
  2. Form the dough: In a large bowl combine the flour and salt. Pour in the yeast mixture and the olive oil (if using). Mix until a rough dough forms.
  3. Knead: Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. First rise: Place dough in a clean bowl, cover, and let rise until doubled. Options:
    • 1–2 hours at room temperature, or
    • 12–24 hours in the fridge for better flavor (cold ferment).

2. Prepare the Sauce

  1. Combine the crushed tomatoes, a pinch of salt, 1 tbsp olive oil, and oregano (optional) in a bowl.
  2. Do not cook the sauce — use it fresh so it stays bright.

3. Prepare Toppings

Slice or tear the mozzarella into small pieces, wash and dry basil, and have olive oil and Parmesan ready.

4. Shape the Pizza

  1. Preheat: Heat your oven as hot as it will go (250–300°C / 475–550°F). Place a pizza stone or steel inside if you have one.
  2. Divide: Remove the dough, divide into 2 equal balls, and let rest 10 minutes.
  3. Stretch: On a lightly floured surface press from the center outward and gently stretch the dough, leaving a slightly thicker edge for the crust.

5. Assemble

  1. Place the stretched dough on baking paper or a floured peel.
  2. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce (avoid over-saucing).
  3. Distribute mozzarella pieces evenly and sprinkle Parmesan if using.
  4. Drizzle a little olive oil and add a pinch of black pepper if desired.

6. Bake

  1. Slide the pizza onto the hot stone or baking tray.
  2. Bake 7–12 minutes until cheese is melted and crust is golden with small charred spots.

7. Finish & Serve

  1. Add fresh basil leaves after the pizza comes out of the oven.
  2. Drizzle extra virgin olive oil, slice, and enjoy!

Variations & Tips

  • Want a quicker dough? Use warm water and a little extra yeast and proof for 1–2 hours only.
  • For a pan-style pizza, press the dough into a well-oiled round pan and bake at 220–230°C for 18–25 minutes.
  • For extra flavor, cold ferment the dough in the fridge for 24–72 hours.
